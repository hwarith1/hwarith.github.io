Research

My research focuses on the intersection of political development, education policy, and social mobilization in post-colonial contexts. Through spatial analysis and historical data, I examine how colonial legacies continue to shape contemporary political dynamics across Africa, Asia, and the Middle East.

Dissertation Research

Political Cleavage Formation After Independence in Africa and Asia

My dissertation political cleavage formation after independence in Africa and Asia. I argue that in these contexts' secondary education, almost nonexistent under colonial rule, and scarcely provided thereafter, formed the basis of enduring political divisions.

My empirical findings show that schools were withheld from regions with a history of support for anti-regime rebellions. As a consequence of this process, meritocracies were manufactured, upward mobility, social status, and regionalized marginalizations were assigned unevenly across national space.

I map thousands of anti-colonial nationalist protests and then look to see whether specific protest waves can be assigned to specific mobilizing factions. I then leverage spatial patterns of protest as a measure of support for specific parties. In Tunisia, I provide a novel spatial measure of support for the Youssefist Revolt, repressed violently by French and Tunisian troops.

I combine this protest data with schooling records in North African cases. In Tunisia, I obtained the complete record of schools (N=6027) built since 1881 and disaggregated by era of construction (Colonial-era, under Bourguiba, after Bourguiba). My results consistently suggest that secondary schools were withheld from Youssefist zones and that subsequently these deprived geographies became opportunity zones for subsequent challenging mobilization. Early protests during Tunisia's Arab Spring revolt were concentrated in Youssefist districts; since the 1980s, Islamists worked to build a base of Support in the Youssefist Center-South.

Current Research Expansion: In Morocco I show persisting inequalities emerging after the Rif War. I am collecting analogous data in Senegal, Cameroon, Algeria and expanding the scope of my investigation to critical colonial cases in French West Africa and across the MENA region.
